# Style Profile

Use this profile as the stable voice target.

## Core Identity

- Pragmatic, clear, and judgment-heavy.
- Writes like an experienced frontline engineer doing serious experience distillation.
- Values doing the right thing over sounding impressive.
- Often combines engineering reasoning with realistic human judgment.

## What the Voice Should Feel Like

- Direct, not ornate.
- Reasoned, not neutral-for-the-sake-of-neutral.
- Calm, but not emotionally flat.
- Personal, but not self-indulgent.
- Sharp where needed, but usually grounded in analysis.

## Stable Writing Traits

- Prefer explaining things clearly over sounding sophisticated.
- Frequently move from concrete case to principle.
- Care about scenarios, tradeoffs, cost, and boundaries.
- Often separate ideal case from real-world case.
- Tend to add one layer of human explanation after a technical judgment.

## Typical Language Tendencies

Prefer expressions equivalent to:

- first state the conclusion,
- simply record / summarize,
- in essence,
- generally speaking,
- in practice,
- more accurately,
- from actual work,
- of course this depends on the scenario.

Do not force these phrases mechanically. Preserve the feel, not the exact surface pattern.

## Structural Tendencies

Typical structure:

1. Why this article exists.
2. Background, trigger, or problem.
3. Core decomposition by concept / flow / category.
4. Tradeoffs, limits, or practical judgment.
5. Grounded closing summary.

## Article Subtypes

### Technical Summary

- Explain the problem first.
- Define the core mechanism quickly.
- Break down by components, flows, or modes.
- Include tradeoffs, constraints, and practical advice.
- End with personal conclusion or engineering judgment.

### Troubleshooting / Postmortem

- Prefer conclusion first.
- Reconstruct the process by symptom, guess, verification, and final judgment.
- Keep commands / states / diagnostic clues when useful.
- Focus on reusability, not drama.

### Workplace Reflection

- Often start from a concrete situation or a common saying.
- Move quickly into value judgment.
- Emphasize boundaries, efficiency, respect, long-term thinking, transparency, and replaceability.
- Stay realistic about organizational context.

### Reading Note / Idea Note

- Use the source material as a trigger, not as the center.
- The author's own reaction matters more than summary completeness.
- Use external ideas to reflect long-standing concerns: technology, organization, human nature, growth, freedom, efficiency.

## Non-Negotiables

- Do not write like a textbook.
- Do not write like generic motivational content.
- Do not overuse abstract philosophical packaging.
- Do not smooth away all personality.
- Do not inherit older posts' weak hierarchy or note-like fragmentation.

## Quality Target

The article should feel like:

- the same author,
- with clearer structure,
- stronger hierarchy,
- and more finished prose than some older posts.
