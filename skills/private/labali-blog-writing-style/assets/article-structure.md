# Article Structure Skeletons

Use the matching skeleton as structural scaffolding for the identified subtype.
Fill with the author's content; do not copy the skeleton text verbatim.

---

## Subtype: Technical Summary

A skill or tool learned, a system understood, or a process debugged.

```
[1–2 sentence lead-in: what the thing is and why it matters to the author now]

## 是什么 / 背景

[Context: what the tech is, where it fits, what problem it solves]

## 怎么用 / 核心用法

[Practical usage: key steps, commands, configs, or decision points]
[Use prose primarily; use code blocks or short lists only where they add precision]

## 坑 / 注意点

[Real issues encountered; tradeoffs observed; things that differ from the docs]

## 小结

[One or two sentences: personal judgment on when/whether to use it]
```

---

## Subtype: Troubleshooting / Postmortem

An incident, a bug, or a production issue traced and resolved.

```
[1–2 sentence lead-in: what broke and when]

## 现象

[Observable symptoms: what the user/system saw]

## 排查过程

[Steps taken, hypotheses tested, dead ends hit — honest trace, not sanitized]

## 根因

[The actual cause, stated plainly]

## 修复

[What was changed and why it fixed it]

## 复盘

[What this reveals about the system, team, or process; what to do differently]
```

---

## Subtype: Workplace Reflection

An observation about work, teams, management, or personal growth.

```
[1–2 sentence lead-in: the situation or trigger for the reflection]

## 观察到的现象

[Specific concrete thing that happened or was noticed — not abstract]

## 为什么会这样

[The author's analysis: structural, behavioral, or incentive-level reasons]

## 自己的判断

[Personal stance: what the author believes is right or wrong, and why]
[This is where the author's voice is most important — avoid hedging]

## 结语

[Grounded close: what the author will do, watch, or accept going forward]
[No forced uplift; no motivational summary]
```

---

## Subtype: Reading Note / Idea Note

A book, article, talk, or idea that left an impression.

```
[1–2 sentence lead-in: what the source is and the author's initial reaction]

## 核心观点 / 让我印象深刻的地方

[The idea or argument that struck the author — paraphrased, not quoted at length]

## 为什么值得记

[Why this idea is interesting, surprising, or useful to the author specifically]
[Tie to the author's own context where natural]

## 保留的疑问 / 不同意的地方

[Honest friction: where the author is unsure, skeptical, or disagrees]
[Skip if none; do not manufacture artificial pushback]

## 结语

[One-sentence takeaway or what the author plans to do with this]
```
